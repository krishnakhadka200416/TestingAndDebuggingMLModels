Testing and Debugging

The training data may contain errors. In particular, some instances may have corrupted data or may be mislabeled. This could happen because mistakes could be made during the data collection or labeling process. Since the training data are considered the ground truth by the learning algorithm, detecting errors in the training data is critically important to ensure model quality.

In order to detect possible incorrect training data, identify the training instances that are influential on incorrect model decision. One approach to identify the buggy influential instances can be performed by influential analysis.








Influential Instances

Machine learning models are ultimately a product of training data and deleting some influential training instances can affect the resulting model. We call the training instances "influential" when their deletion from the training data considerably changes the parameters or predictions of the model. By identifying the influential training instances, we can "debug" the machine learning models and better explain their behaviors and predictions.

Influential Analysis

Influential analysis can be performed using influential functions, which uses a concept called upweighting to approximate the influence of an instance. Upweighting allows parameter changes to be computed without retraining the model. Influential functions require differentiable models and can still be expensive to compute for large models.
