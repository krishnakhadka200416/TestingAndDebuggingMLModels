Testing and Debugging ML Models



Machine Learning techniques are used to perform data-driven decision-making. The machine learning models deal with voluminous amounts of data to train the model. The training data may contain errors that lead to wrong predictions for some testing data. In particular, some instances may have corrupted data or may be mislabeled. This could happen because mistakes could be made during the data collection or labeling process.

Since the training data are considered the ground truth by the learning algorithm, detecting errors in the training data is critically important to ensure model quality. To detect possible incorrect training data, identifying the training instances that are influential on an incorrect model decision is important.

Debugging ML models is harder than debugging conventional programs. An ML codebase has a lot more moving elements than a typical codebase. There are datasets, fine-tuned model weights during training, optimization and its gradients changing during training, and so on. Monitoring and intervening during ML training is difficult. Because ML code is mostly based on higher-level frameworks that remove underlying complexities, models are difficult to debug.

An approach to testing and debugging ML models involves identifying influential buggy training data and changing their labels. The model is then retrained to give correct predictions.

This website consists of related papers, approaches, and software tools for ML model testing and debugging purposes laying a foundation for better debugging approaches.
