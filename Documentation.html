Research Documentation

Research Documentation helps to identify the research questions that have previously been explored. It also helps to develop a research question, aim or hypothesis that is clear, focused and related to the topic of interest. It helps to uncover all relevant knowledge related to the topic or research questions.

Search Process
  1. Identify a topic of interest and spend time identifying keywords.
  2. Using keywords conduct a search of relevant topics.
  3. Review all references sourced and retrieve a copy of relevant references.
  4. Read all relevant material sourced and identify new references through citations.
  5. Organize all material in preparation for analysis and integration in the review.

Commonly used Databases for Search

  1. IEEE Xplore Digital Library - 4.7+ million articles
  2. ACM Digital Library - 2.8+ million articles
  3. DBLP Computer Science Bibliography - 4.3 million articles
  4. Microsoft Academic - 13.9 million articles
  5. Springer Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) - 415,000+ articles
  6. Google Scholar
